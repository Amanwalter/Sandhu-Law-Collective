ABOUT THE FIRM

Sandhu Law Collective (SLC)

Sandhu Law Collective (SLC) is a boutique litigation and advisory firm founded in 2014 to provide prosecution, advisory, litigation and enforcement services across diverse areas of law. Based in the Chandigarh Capital Region, the firm’s core practice centres around the Hon’ble Punjab and Haryana High Court, while also appearing before the Hon’ble Supreme Court of India, various High Courts, Tribunals, District Courts and statutory authorities.

Although relatively young in years, the firm has steadily built a reputation for competence, courtroom discipline and strategic depth. It is recognised for structured pleadings, focused advocacy and the ability to handle complex matters with clarity and precision. Professional standards and confidentiality form the bedrock of the practice. Every engagement is approached with discretion, integrity and thorough preparation, ensuring that client trust remains paramount.

SLC endeavours to provide its clients with cogent, practical and tailor-made advice delivered within efficient timelines followed by taking action in the form of doing cases. The objective is not merely dispute resolution, but advancing the broader legal and business goals of those it represents.

The firm functions as a collaborative chamber where client interest remains the primary driving force. It continues to operate as a litigation-centric chamber committed to preparation, discretion and principled advocacy, with confidentiality and professional responsibility forming an integral part of its institutional ethos.

LEADERSHIP

Leadership Team

Avtar Singh Sandhu

Former Director of Prosecution & Litigation, Government of Punjab

Former Additional Advocate General, Punjab

Mr. Avtar Singh Sandhu served as Director of Prosecution & Litigation and Legal Advisor to the Government of Punjab from 2008 until his retirement in 2017, and subsequently as Additional Advocate General, Punjab before the Punjab and Haryana High Court.

He brings to SLC decades of experience in state prosecutions, governmental advisory work and institutional litigation, which continues to guide the firm’s approach to complex criminal and public law matters.

Harparteek Singh Sandhu

(H.P.S. Sandhu)

Advocate | hps@sandhulawcollective.com

Mr. H.P.S. Sandhu practices across civil, service and regulatory matters, with primary focus on criminal law. Matters handled in his practice have resulted in significant decisions before the Hon’ble Supreme Court of India and the Hon’ble Punjab and Haryana High Court.

He has appeared before the Patna, Karnataka and Bombay High Courts and various District Courts across Punjab and Haryana. His work includes extensive litigation under the Indian Penal Code, the Drugs and Cosmetics Act and the POCSO Act, with an emphasis on careful preparation and disciplined courtroom advocacy.

Avin Kaur Sandhu

Advocate

avin@sandhulawcollective.com

Ms. Avin Kaur Sandhu has over 15 years of experience. She began her legal career at Dua Associates, New Delhi, working in international trade and export laws before moving to a litigation practice at Chandigarh.

Her practice spans constitutional and service law, land and property disputes, matrimonial litigation and alternate dispute resolution. She is Standing Counsel and Legal Retainer for GMADA and also represents Swiss nationals before the courts at Chandigarh in coordination with the Lawyer of Confidence, Embassy of Switzerland in India.

Confidentiality & Attorney–Client Privilege

SLC adheres to the highest standards of professional confidentiality. All communications, instructions, documents, strategies and discussions shared by clients are strictly protected under the attorney–client privilege and are never disclosed to any third party without explicit consent.

This privilege forms the foundation of our practice and ensures complete trust, candour and security in every engagement. However, information that is already in the public domain or independently reported through online platforms, media, or official records lies outside the scope of this privilege.
